Privacy

Privacy-first by design.

Bank Harm Registry is designed to collect only what is needed for private intake, consent, routing, moderation, and support matching. It does not automatically publish consumer stories.

What may be collected

Name or alias, contact email, institution, issue, state, timeline, evidence description, requested remedy, consent choices, and submission metadata.

Where it may go

Depending on configuration, a private email inbox and/or private GitHub issue queue. A public GitHub repository must never be used for private intake.

What stays private

Names, emails, raw stories, documents, and contact details are not published automatically. Public summaries require separate consent, redaction, and review.

Do not submit

  • Social Security numbers
  • Full account or card numbers
  • Login credentials or PINs
  • Full dates of birth
  • Unredacted medical or identity documents

Retention and deletion

Before launch, configure a privacy contact email, define a written retention schedule, and document how consumers can request access, correction, or deletion. Submission receipts stored on a device contain metadata only and can be cleared through the browser.

Launch requirement

Replace all placeholder contact information and obtain qualified legal/privacy review before collecting sensitive real-world reports at scale.
